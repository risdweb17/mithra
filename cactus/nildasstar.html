Nilda's Star Cactus


Star cactus is very small, flat and spineless cactus that blooms from March through May. Its flowers are yellow with orange throats. It grows in sparsely vegetated areas with saline clays or loams in the Rio Grande Plains. Although the cactus has no spines, it has some chemical compounds and clusters of short white wool that cover and protects it. Star Cactus grew in Cameron, Starr and Hidalgo Counties in Texas and in Nuevo Leon and Tamaulipas in Mexico. In the present, it only grows in Starr county, Texas. Although it is almost eliminated in Texas, it is still abundant in Mexico. This species can be cultivated from seed if given the appropriate sandy conditions. The San Antonio Botanical Garden is working with this technique in order to propagate it.
